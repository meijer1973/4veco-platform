## Operationalized sprint procedure

1. Establish baseline: graph exists, graph integrity passes, 31 edges exist, 13 main edges are pending, and 0 edges are human-approved.
2. Prepare a review packet that samples:
   - all prerequisite edges
   - D04 unit-design support edge
   - diagnostic exam-gap edge
   - term-definition edge
   - policy/source-ranking edge
3. Run bounded reviews for:
   - data integrity: IDs, edge statuses, traceability, generated-report handling
   - evidence quality: source strength and whether evidence supports each edge
   - pedagogy: whether the graph edge is appropriate as a teaching prerequisite/support relationship
4. Conduct calibration questions interactively. Ask one question at a time and include enough context that the reviewer does not need to look up IDs.
5. Record each human answer in `human-interview.md` immediately after the answer is given.
6. Analyze answer patterns after the first calibration set and choose either targeted follow-ups or a closure proposal.
7. Ask targeted follow-ups for any risky edge group, especially D04, macro wage rigidity, D16, diagnostic edges, or elasticity routing.
8. After the answers and follow-ups, propose one gate closure:
   - `pass`: graph may be used by later internal systems as approved
   - `pass_with_conditions`: graph may be used only under named conditions or edge subsets
   - `hold`: graph remains draft; R6/R7/R10 consumers may not treat it as approved
   - `fail`: graph is rejected and must be rebuilt
9. Ask for explicit human confirmation of the closure proposal.
10. Only after explicit confirmation, write `gate-closure.json`, result files, and roadmap completion state.
11. Stop if the human reviewer does not confirm a closure proposal. Do not infer approval from "continue" or "OK".
